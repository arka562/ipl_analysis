# IPL Intelligence: Key Insights

## Dataset

- Seasons covered: 2007–2026.
- Matches analysed: 1,239.
- Total runs: 811,453; total wickets: 29,783.

## Executive Summary

1. IPL scoring has not been static: the highest run-rate season was 2026 at 9.83 runs per over, compared with the lowest season, 2013, at 7.68.
2. The strongest winner-loser phase gap is the middle phase: winners averaged 83.54 runs and losers averaged 74.98, a gap of 8.56 runs.
3. Toss advantage varies heavily by season: toss winners peaked at 61.4% in 2025 and fell as low as 43.7% in 2024.
4. Chasing was strongest in 2016, with chasing teams winning 68.3% of completed matches; it was weakest in 2015 at 42.9%.
5. Among teams with at least 50 matches, Gujarat Titans has the highest win percentage at 62.2%.
6. Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium, New Chandigarh has the highest average first-innings score among venues with at least five matches: 217.
7. Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium, New Chandigarh is the venue where toss winners had the strongest match win percentage: 80.0%.
8. The all-time top run scorer is V Kohli with 9,228 runs.
9. The all-time top wicket taker is YS Chahal with 233 wickets.
10. In death overs, AB de Villiers stands out for a 235.26 strike rate among the top phase run scorers, while DJ Bravo leads death-over wickets with 102.

## Why This Matters

This project is not just a dashboard. It is an analytics pipeline that starts with raw nested JSON, creates normalized ball-by-ball tables, loads them into a SQL warehouse, exports reusable report tables, and turns those reports into portfolio-ready findings.

## Resume Framing

Built an end-to-end IPL analytics platform using 1,200+ raw Cricsheet JSON files from 2008-present, transforming 294K+ ball-by-ball deliveries into normalized datasets, a DuckDB analytics warehouse, SQL report tables, and insight reports covering toss impact, phase-wise scoring, venue behavior, team performance, and player value.
